About PSC

Penang Science Cluster (PSC) (formerly known as Penang Science Council) is an industry-led initiative in collaboration with the State Government. It was initiated by YAB Chief Minister in 2009 and formally incorporated 2012. .

Our first centre was in Krystal Point, with the first workshop starting on 14 December 2013, and was officially opened by Penang’s Chief Minister on 18 April 2014. Now we have our 13 000 sq. ft. main centre, PSC@Heritage, at the Wisma Yeap Chor Ee building in Georgetown that was launched on 5 December 2015. The entire ground floor is provided rent-free to PSC by Penang Development Corporation.

Scratch

What if you want to create a game with coding but you don’t know where to start?

Scratch 3.0 allows you to easily dive into the realm of coding by representing code graphically in an intuitive way

Scratch is designed, developed, and moderated by the Scratch Foundation, a nonprofit organization. It is provided free of charge.



What you can do with Scratch?

​




You can create your own interactive stories,animations and share your creations with others.




You can create and share Scratch projects and learn to think creatively.




With the provided visuals, programmers can create animations, text, stories and more.










Questions about Scratch


Scratch is available free of charge. You can use it in your school, and you can teach a course about it (even a course that costs money).

With ScratchJr, young children (ages 5-7) learn important new skills as they program their own interactive stories and games. By snapping together graphical programming blocks, children can make characters move, jump, dance, and sing.

You can get into Scratch on your own easily, but sometimes having step-by-step directions to building help jumpstart your learning.

There's no way to truly 'master' Scratch, seeing as even scratchers like @TheLogFather and @griffpatch are discovering new things that can be done with Scratch.

The stage is basically the background of your project. Like sprites, the stage can have different costumes that change as the story plays out. The stage window is the main area where the action of your program takes place.

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality (often just called VR) is the name for computer technology that makes a person feel like they are somewhere else.

It uses software to produce images, sounds, and other sensations to create a different place so that a user feels like he or she is really part of this other place


​

Interesting Facts about Virtual Reality

​

VR technology is now being used in healthcare to treat depression, anxiety and PTSD amongst other things..


53% of consumers say they are more likely to purchase from a brand that sponsors a VR experience.


81% of people who try virtual reality tell their friends about it.






Questions about Virtual Reality

VR has the ability to rewire the brain and enhance neural connections that are needed for learning and memory. In a simulated environment, the brain is seeing and doing exactly what is required – it is not filling in the gaps..

VR in Mental Health.

VR technology has become a primary method for treating post-traumatic stress.

It has also been used to treat anxiety, phobias and depression.

Non-immersive reality

This type can be seen in the virtual reality flight simulator. It has a widescreen PC with a surround system and comes with other accessories like headphones, joysticks etc.

Fully immersive reality

One would experience full virtual experience because this type of virtual reality comes with a very powerful computer.
